Members of Queen

Freddie Mercury

Freddy Mercury was born on September 5, 1946 in Stone Town, Zanzibar (Tanzania). His birthname is Farrokh Bulsara and eventually changed his name to Freddie Mercury when he joined the band. Freddie attended in the Ealing College of Art and has joined multiple bands before Queen. He is the vocalist and songwriter for Queen. Freddie is the mastermind behind the band and even designd the logo. He was influenced by "Smile" and the music style of rock. He passed away on November 24, 1991 in Kensington, London due to aids.

Roger Taylor

Roger Taylor was born on July 26, 1949 in King's Lynn, England. He is the singer, guitarist, drummer for the band. He moved to London to study in dentistry and biology but eventually pursued in a music career. He joined the band, Smile along with Brain May and eventually transitioned to Queen, with Freddie Mercury and has even became a solo artist at the time and even formed a group called The Cross. After Freddie's death, he continues to stay active in the band.

Brian May

Brian May was born on July 19, 1947 in Hampton, England. He is the lead guitarist and vocalist for the band. Previously, he was a former member of the original band, Smile before transitioning to what is now Queen. He studied in Hampton Grammar School, and enrolled in London Imperial College to study for his bachelors in Science. He would then study for his PH.D in 1974 and completed his degree by 2007 due to being an active member of the band.

John Deacon

John Deacon was born on August 19, 1951 in Leicester, England. He enrolled in the Chelsea College to study a degree involving electronics. In 1970, he met Brian May and Roger Taylor where he auditioned to be a part of a member of the band, Queen. He became the bassist and even songwriter of the band. He was the last member to be recruited and eventually carry the band's lineup till Freddie's death. After Freddie's death, John retired from the band to be with his wife and children in London.
